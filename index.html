SQS — Structural Intelligence Quotient Self-Test

Structural intelligence describes how the mind builds, adapts, and navigates systems. SQS focuses on structural thinking and synthesis, not traditional IQ. This is a self-assessment for personal insight, not a clinical or standardized psychological tool.

1) Fractal Learning

Instant integration of new knowledge into an existing mental framework.

I rapidly connect new information to what I already know.

When learning, I map patterns across topics automatically.

I integrate examples into general rules quickly.

I notice structural similarities between new and old problems.

2) Recursive Reasoning

Ability to iterate and refine systems without losing coherence.

I refine my plans iteratively without losing clarity.

I revisit earlier steps to improve the whole system.

I debug processes by looping through causes and effects.

I can trace feedback loops and adjust accordingly.

3) Multidomain Flexibility

Smooth switching and integration across multiple domains.

I switch between different fields without losing context.

I borrow methods from one domain to solve another.

I explain concepts using analogies across disciplines.

I merge inputs from varied sources into one picture.

4) System Creation

Capacity to design entirely new frameworks, architectures, and models.

I design frameworks that others can reuse.

I create templates to streamline repeated tasks.

I organize ideas into architectures before executing.

I document structures so they can be scaled.

5) Parallel Hypothesis Management

Maintaining multiple working models or narratives at once.

I keep multiple explanations active until evidence decides.

I run small experiments to compare competing ideas.

I track alternative scenarios without confusion.

I update probabilities as new information arrives.

6) Strategic Structural Cognition

High-level planning, long-horizon thinking, and architecture of actions.

I plan projects with layered milestones and dependencies.

I anticipate downstream effects of current decisions.

I design contingency paths for critical steps.

I align daily actions with long-term architectures.
